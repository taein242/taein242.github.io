Neural representations of neuropsychiatric symptoms in Alzheimer's disease continuum

Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is characterized not only by memory impairment but also by neuropsychiatric symptoms (NPS), including affective and behavioral disturbances that significantly impact patients and their caregivers.

What drives heterogeneity of emotional and behavioral patterns in AD patients?

To address this question, I utilized positron emission tomography (PET) and functional magnetic resonance imaging. (fMRI) By integrating pathological markers with functional connectivity analyses, this project aimed to provide a multilevel understanding of NPS in AD.
